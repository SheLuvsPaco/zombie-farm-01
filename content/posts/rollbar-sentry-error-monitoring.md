# Rollbar vs Sentry: Which is Better for Error Monitoring?

## Quick Verdict
For small to medium-sized teams with a limited budget, Rollbar is a more cost-effective option, offering a free plan and a pricing model that scales with the number of users. However, for larger teams or those requiring more advanced features, Sentry's extensive integration options and customizable workflows make it a better choice. Ultimately, the decision between Rollbar and Sentry depends on your team's specific needs and use case.

## When to Choose Rollbar
* If you're a 10-person startup with a limited budget and need a simple, easy-to-use error monitoring tool, Rollbar's free plan and affordable pricing make it an attractive option.
* If you're already using a smaller set of tools and don't require a wide range of integrations, Rollbar's more streamlined interface may be a better fit.
* If you're a 50-person SaaS company needing to monitor errors for a small to medium-sized application, Rollbar's pricing model and features should suffice.
* For example, if you're building a mobile app with a small team and limited resources, Rollbar's ease of use and affordability make it a great choice.

## When to Choose Sentry
* If you're a 100-person enterprise with a large, complex application and require advanced features like customizable workflows and extensive integration options, Sentry's scalability and support make it a better choice.
* If you're already using a large set of tools and require a wide range of integrations, Sentry's extensive integration options make it a better fit.
* If you're a 20-person team with a growing application and need to monitor errors in real-time, Sentry's advanced features and support make it a better choice.
* For example, if you're building a large e-commerce platform with multiple teams and require advanced error monitoring features, Sentry's customizable workflows and extensive integration options make it a great choice.

## Real-World Use Case: Error Monitoring
Let's say you're a 50-person SaaS company with a medium-sized application, and you need to monitor errors in real-time. With Rollbar, setup complexity is relatively low, taking around 2-3 hours to integrate with your application. Ongoing maintenance burden is also relatively low, requiring around 1-2 hours per week to review and resolve errors. The cost breakdown for 100 users/actions would be around $600 per month. However, with Sentry, setup complexity is higher, taking around 5-7 hours to integrate with your application, and ongoing maintenance burden is also higher, requiring around 3-5 hours per week to review and resolve errors. The cost breakdown for 100 users/actions would be around $2,600 per month. Common gotchas with both tools include underestimating the time required for setup and maintenance, as well as not fully utilizing the advanced features available.

## Migration Considerations
If switching between Rollbar and Sentry, data export/import limitations are a significant consideration. Rollbar allows for easy export of error data, but Sentry's data export options are more limited. Training time needed to switch between tools is around 2-5 hours, depending on the complexity of your application and the features you're using. Hidden costs to consider include the cost of re-integrating with other tools and the potential need for additional support or consulting services.

## FAQ
Q: Which tool is better for small teams with limited budgets?
A: Rollbar is generally a better choice for small teams with limited budgets, offering a free plan and a pricing model that scales with the number of users.

Q: Can I use both Rollbar and Sentry together?
A: Yes, you can use both Rollbar and Sentry together, but it may require additional setup and configuration to integrate the two tools. It's generally more practical to choose one tool and use it as your primary error monitoring solution.

Q: Which tool has better ROI for Error Monitoring?
A: Based on a 12-month projection, Rollbar's pricing model and features provide a better ROI for small to medium-sized teams, with a potential cost savings of up to 50% compared to Sentry. However, for larger teams or those requiring more advanced features, Sentry's customizable workflows and extensive integration options may provide a better ROI in the long run.

**Bottom Line:** Ultimately, the choice between Rollbar and Sentry depends on your team's specific needs and use case, but for small to medium-sized teams with limited budgets, Rollbar's cost-effective pricing model and easy-to-use interface make it a great choice for error monitoring.
