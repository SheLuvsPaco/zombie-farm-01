# Loom vs Glide: Which is Better for Video Messages?

## Quick Verdict
For teams of 10-50 people with a budget of $100-500 per month, Loom is a better choice for video messages due to its ease of use and robust feature set. However, for larger teams or those with more complex async communication needs, Glide's scalability and customization options make it a more suitable option. Ultimately, the choice between Loom and Glide depends on your team's specific needs and use case.

## When to Choose Loom
* If you're a 10-person startup needing a simple and easy-to-use video messaging tool, Loom is a great choice due to its low cost and ease of use.
* If you're a 20-person marketing team needing to create and share video messages with clients, Loom's robust feature set and integrations with popular marketing tools make it a good fit.
* If you're a 50-person SaaS company needing to reduce sync time for remote teams, Loom's video messaging features can help reduce sync time from 15 minutes to 30 seconds.
* If you're a small team with a limited budget, Loom's pricing model is more transparent and cost-effective.

## When to Choose Glide
* If you're a 100-person enterprise needing a customizable and scalable video messaging tool, Glide's enterprise features and support make it a better choice.
* If you're a 50-person sales team needing to create and share video messages with customers, Glide's customizable video templates and workflows can help improve sales conversions.
* If you're a large team with complex async communication needs, Glide's customizable workflows and integrations with popular CRM tools make it a good fit.
* If you're a team with a large budget and needing a high level of support, Glide's 24/7 phone and email support is more comprehensive.
