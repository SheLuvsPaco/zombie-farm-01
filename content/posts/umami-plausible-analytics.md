# Umami vs Plausible: Which is Better for Analytics?

## Quick Verdict
For small to medium-sized teams with a budget under $100/month, Umami is a more cost-effective self-hosted option with a simpler setup process. However, for larger teams or those requiring more advanced features, Plausible's scalability and support make it a better choice. Ultimately, the decision depends on your team's specific needs and priorities.

## When to Choose Umami
* If you're a 10-person startup with a simple website and limited budget, Umami's free and open-source model can help you get started with analytics quickly.
* If you prioritize data ownership and don't want to rely on third-party services, Umami's self-hosted option ensures you have full control over your data.
* If you're already familiar with Umami's technology stack (e.g., Node.js, PostgreSQL), you can leverage your existing expertise to set up and maintain the platform.
* For example, if you're a 50-person SaaS company needing basic analytics for your marketing website, Umami can provide a cost-effective solution with minimal setup complexity.

## When to Choose Plausible
* If you're a 100-person e-commerce company with a high-traffic website, Plausible's scalability and support can ensure your analytics setup can handle large volumes of data.
* If you require advanced analytics features like funnels, heatmaps, or A/B testing, Plausible's paid plans offer more comprehensive capabilities.
* If you prioritize ease of use and don't want to spend time setting up and maintaining your analytics platform, Plausible's user-friendly interface and priority support can simplify the process.
* For instance, if you're a 200-person enterprise with multiple websites and complex analytics requirements, Plausible's advanced features and support can provide a more robust solution.

## Real-World Use Case: Analytics
Let's say you're a 20-person marketing agency with a website that receives 10,000 page views per month. You want to set up analytics to track basic metrics like page views, bounce rate, and referral traffic.
* Setup complexity: Umami requires 2-3 hours to set up, while Plausible takes 4-6 hours.
* Ongoing maintenance burden: Umami requires occasional updates and backups, while Plausible handles maintenance automatically.
* Cost breakdown for 100 users/actions: Umami is free, while Plausible costs $5/month (based on 10,000 page views).
* Common gotchas: With Umami, you need to ensure your server can handle the analytics data, while with Plausible, you need to monitor your page view limits to avoid additional costs.

## Migration Considerations
If switching between Umami and Plausible:
* Data export/import limitations: Umami allows full data export, while Plausible has limitations on data export for free plans.
* Training time needed: 1-2 hours to learn the new platform's interface and features.
* Hidden costs: With Plausible, you may incur additional costs if you exceed your page view limits or require priority support.
