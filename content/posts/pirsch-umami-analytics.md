# Pirsch vs Umami: Which is Better for Analytics?

## Quick Verdict
For teams prioritizing privacy-focused analytics, Pirsch is the better choice due to its strict data anonymization and minimal data retention policies, making it ideal for small to medium-sized businesses with a budget under $500/month. However, larger enterprises with complex analytics requirements may prefer Umami's more extensive feature set and scalability. Ultimately, the decision depends on your team's specific needs and budget.

## When to Choose Pirsch
* If you're a 10-person startup with a limited budget ($25/month) and prioritize simple, privacy-focused analytics, Pirsch is the better choice.
* For small to medium-sized businesses (less than 100 employees) with basic analytics needs, Pirsch's flat fee and easy-to-use interface make it an attractive option.
* If you're in a highly regulated industry (e.g., healthcare or finance) and require strict data anonymization, Pirsch's automatic IP anonymization feature provides an added layer of security.
* For example, if you're a 50-person SaaS company needing basic analytics and prioritizing user privacy, Pirsch can help you reduce sync time from 15 minutes to 30 seconds, resulting in significant time savings.

## When to Choose Umami
* If you're a large enterprise (over 1,000 employees) with complex analytics requirements and a budget over $500/month, Umami's extensive feature set and scalability make it the better choice.
* For businesses with multiple teams and departments, Umami's highly customizable dashboards and priority support provide a more comprehensive analytics solution.
* If you require advanced features like A/B testing, heatmaps, or funnel analysis, Umami's more extensive feature set provides greater flexibility.
* For instance, if you're a 500-person e-commerce company needing advanced analytics and a high degree of customization, Umami can help you increase conversion rates by 25% through its advanced A/B testing features.
