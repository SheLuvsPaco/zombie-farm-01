# Alfred vs Raycast: Which is Better for Mac Launcher?

## Quick Verdict
For small to medium-sized teams with a budget under $100 per user, Alfred is a more cost-effective option with a steeper learning curve. However, for larger teams or those prioritizing ease of use, Raycast offers a more streamlined experience with a higher price point. Ultimately, the choice between Alfred and Raycast depends on your team's specific needs and budget.

## When to Choose Alfred
- If you're a 10-person startup with a limited budget and need a high degree of customization, Alfred's one-time payment model and extensive integrations make it a more attractive option.
- For power users who require advanced workflows and scripting capabilities, Alfred's steep learning curve is worth the investment.
- If you're a solo developer or freelancer, Alfred's cost-effective pricing and extensive community support make it an ideal choice.
- For example, if you're a 50-person SaaS company needing to automate complex workflows and integrate with multiple third-party tools, Alfred's customization options and scalability make it a better fit.

## When to Choose Raycast
- If you're a large enterprise with a budget over $1,000 per month and prioritize ease of use, Raycast's streamlined interface and priority support make it a more suitable option.
- For teams with limited technical expertise, Raycast's gentle learning curve and extensive documentation make it easier to onboard new users.
- If you're a fast-growing startup that needs to quickly scale your Mac launcher solution, Raycast's support for up to 10,000 users and robust app management features make it a better choice.
- For instance, if you're a 200-person marketing agency needing to manage multiple apps and workflows across different teams, Raycast's scalability and ease of use make it a more attractive option.

## Real-World Use Case: Mac Launcher
Setting up Alfred as a Mac launcher takes around 2-3 days, with an ongoing maintenance burden of 1-2 hours per week.
