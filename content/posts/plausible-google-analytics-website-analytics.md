# Plausible vs Google Analytics: Which is Better for Website Analytics?

## Quick Verdict
For teams prioritizing privacy-focused tracking, Plausible is the better choice, offering a more straightforward and cost-effective solution. However, larger teams with complex analytics needs may still prefer Google Analytics for its extensive feature set. Ultimately, the decision depends on your team size, budget, and specific use case.

## When to Choose Plausible
* If you're a 10-person startup with a simple website and prioritize data privacy, Plausible's flat fee and easy setup make it an attractive choice.
* If you're a 50-person SaaS company needing to track website analytics without sacrificing user privacy, Plausible's scalable solution and real-time analytics are well-suited.
* If you're a solo entrepreneur with a small budget and limited technical expertise, Plausible's intuitive interface and affordable pricing make it a great option.
* If you're a non-profit organization requiring transparent and privacy-focused tracking, Plausible's commitment to data ownership and minimal data collection align with your values.

## When to Choose Google Analytics
* If you're a large enterprise with complex analytics needs, such as advanced segmentation and e-commerce tracking, Google Analytics' extensive feature set is a better fit.
* If you're a marketing agency managing multiple client websites, Google Analytics' scalability and extensive integration options make it a more practical choice.
* If you're a data-driven business requiring advanced funnel analysis and A/B testing, Google Analytics' advanced features provide more insights.
* If you're a well-established company with a large budget and dedicated analytics team, Google Analytics' 24/7 support and extensive documentation justify the higher cost.

## Migration Considerations
If switching from Google Analytics to Plausible, be aware of the following:
* Data export limitations: Google Analytics allows data export, but it may not be compatible with Plausible's import format.
* Training time needed: 1-2 hours to learn Plausible's interface and features.
* Hidden costs: Potential costs for custom integration or data migration services.
