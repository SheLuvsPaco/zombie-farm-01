# Height vs Linear: Which is Better for Project Management?

## Quick Verdict
For teams with 20+ members and a budget over $1,000/month, Height is the better choice due to its AI-powered task prioritization and advanced project management features. However, smaller teams or those on a tighter budget may find Linear's more affordable pricing and simpler interface a better fit. Ultimately, the decision depends on your specific project management needs and resources.

## When to Choose Height
* If you're a 50-person SaaS company needing advanced project management features, such as AI-powered task prioritization and native Gantt chart support, Height is the better choice.
* For teams with complex project requirements, such as multiple dependencies and critical path management, Height's advanced features provide more value.
* If your team is already using Jira or Trello, Height's native integrations make it a more convenient option.
* For large-scale projects with 100+ tasks and 20+ team members, Height's scalability and support features are essential.

## When to Choose Linear
* If you're a small team of 5-10 members with simple project management needs, Linear's affordable pricing and easy-to-use interface make it a great choice.
* For teams on a tight budget (less than $500/month), Linear's flat fee pricing is more cost-effective.
* If your team is already using Asana or GitHub, Linear's native integrations provide a seamless experience.
* For small-scale projects with fewer than 50 tasks and 10 team members, Linear's simplicity and ease of use are sufficient.

## Real-World Use Case: Project Management
Let's consider a 100-user project with 500 tasks, 20 dependencies, and a 6-month timeline. 
* Setup complexity: Height requires 2-3 days of setup, while Linear takes 1-2 days.

* Hidden costs: Height charges a one-time migration fee of $500, while Linear does not have any hidden costs.
