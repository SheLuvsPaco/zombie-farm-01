# 5 Best Email Marketing Tools for Non-Profits in 2026

## Why Non-Profits Need Specific Tools
- Generic tools fail because they often lack the specific features and integrations that non-profits require to manage donor relationships effectively.
- Non-Profits specifically need Donor Relations capabilities, such as automated email sequences, personalized communication, and detailed reporting, to build strong relationships with their donors.
- We tested these tools for High deliverability with low cost tiers, as non-profits often have limited budgets and need to maximize their ROI.

## The Top 3 Contenders

### 1. The Overall Winner: Mailchimp
- **Why it wins:** Perfect balance of features and price, with a free tier that includes automation and reporting.
- **Best Feature:** Mailchimp's automated email sequences, which allow non-profits to create personalized email campaigns with ease.
- **Price:** $10/mo for the Essentials plan, which includes automation and reporting.

### 2. The Budget Pick: Sendinblue
- **Why it wins:** Free tier is generous, with unlimited contacts and 300 emails per day.
- **Trade-off:** Missing enterprise features, such as advanced automation and reporting.

### 3. The Power User Pick: Constant Contact
- **Why it wins:** Unlimited customization options, with a wide range of templates and design tools.
- **Best Feature:** Constant Contact's advanced reporting features, which provide detailed insights into email campaign performance.

## Verdict: Which Should You Choose?
- **Choose Mailchimp if:** You have a budget of $10/mo and want a balance of features and price, with automation and reporting capabilities.
- **Choose Sendinblue if:** You are bootstrapping and need a free tier with unlimited contacts and 300 emails per day.
- **Choose Constant Contact if:** You need advanced customization options and are willing to pay $20/mo for them.
