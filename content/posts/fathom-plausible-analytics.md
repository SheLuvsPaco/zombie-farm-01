# Fathom vs Plausible: Which is Better for Analytics?

## Quick Verdict
For small to medium-sized teams with a budget under $100/month, Plausible is the better choice due to its simplicity and cost-effectiveness. However, larger teams with more complex analytics needs may prefer Fathom for its advanced features and scalability. Ultimately, the choice between Fathom and Plausible depends on your team's specific use case and requirements.

## When to Choose Fathom
* If you're a 50-person SaaS company needing advanced analytics features like heatmaps and session recording, Fathom is the better choice.
* If you have a large budget (over $100/month) and require phone support, Fathom's enterprise plan is a good option.
* If you need to integrate with multiple third-party tools (e.g. Google Ads, Facebook), Fathom's wider range of integrations makes it a better fit.
* If you have a complex analytics setup with multiple funnels and goals, Fathom's advanced features will help you track and optimize your workflow.

## When to Choose Plausible
* If you're a small team (less than 10 people) with a limited budget (under $50/month), Plausible is a cost-effective and simple solution for basic analytics needs.
* If you prioritize ease of use and don't need advanced features, Plausible's gentle learning curve makes it a great choice.
* If you're looking for a lightweight analytics solution with a small footprint, Plausible is a good option.
* If you're already using other simple tools (e.g. Stripe, Google Analytics) and want to integrate with them, Plausible's limited but focused integrations will work well.
