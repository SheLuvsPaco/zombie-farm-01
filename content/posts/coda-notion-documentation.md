# Coda vs Notion: Which is Better for Documentation?

## Quick Verdict
For teams of 10-50 users with a budget of $1,000-$5,000 per year, Coda is the better choice for documentation due to its robust automation features and scalable pricing model. However, for smaller teams or those with simpler documentation needs, Notion's free plan and user-friendly interface make it a more suitable option. Ultimately, the choice between Coda and Notion depends on the specific documentation requirements and budget of your team.

## When to Choose Coda
- If you're a 50-person SaaS company needing automated documentation workflows, Coda's customizable pricing model and robust automation features make it the better choice.
- For teams with complex documentation requirements, such as conditional formatting and automated table of contents, Coda's advanced features are more suitable.
- If your team has a large budget ($5,000-$10,000 per year) and requires scalable documentation solutions, Coda's enterprise plan is the way to go.
- For example, if you're a 20-person marketing agency with a budget of $2,000 per year, Coda's automation features can help reduce documentation time by 30% and increase team productivity.

## When to Choose Notion
- If you're a small team of 5-10 users with simple documentation needs, Notion's free plan and user-friendly interface make it a more cost-effective option.
- For teams with limited budget ($0-$1,000 per year), Notion's free plan provides a robust set of features, including basic formatting and manual table of contents.
- If your team requires a high degree of customization and flexibility in their documentation workflows, Notion's manual workflows and limited automation capabilities may be more suitable.
- For example, if you're a 10-person startup with a budget of $500 per year, Notion's free plan can provide a basic documentation solution that meets your needs.
