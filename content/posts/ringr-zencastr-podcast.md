# Ringr vs Zencastr: Which is Better for Podcast?

## Quick Verdict
For podcasters who prioritize mobility and ease of use, Ringr is the better choice due to its dedicated mobile app, which allows for remote interviews and recordings on-the-go. However, for larger teams or those with more complex podcast production needs, Zencastr's web-based platform and advanced features may be more suitable. Ultimately, the decision between Ringr and Zencastr depends on your specific podcasting requirements and budget.

## When to Choose Ringr
* If you're a solo podcaster or a small team (2-5 people) with a limited budget ($10-20/month), Ringr's basic plan is a cost-effective option.
* If you prioritize mobility and need to conduct remote interviews or recordings on-the-go, Ringr's mobile app is a significant advantage.
* If you're a beginner podcaster who wants a user-friendly interface and easy setup, Ringr's learning curve is relatively gentle.
* For example, if you're a 10-person marketing team at a small business needing to create a weekly podcast, Ringr's pro plan ($19.99/month) is a good fit.

## When to Choose Zencastr
* If you're a larger team (10-50 people) with a bigger budget ($50-100/month), Zencastr's pro plan offers more advanced features and scalability.
* If you need to integrate your podcasting workflow with other tools like Zoom, Slack, or Google Drive, Zencastr's extensive integrations are a significant advantage.
* If you prioritize high-quality audio and need more advanced features like noise reduction and EQ, Zencastr's audio quality is superior.
* For instance, if you're a 50-person SaaS company needing to create a daily podcast with multiple hosts and guests, Zencastr's pro plan ($40/month) is a better choice.
