# Payload CMS vs Sanity: Which is Better for Headless CMS?

## Quick Verdict
For teams with 10-50 developers and a budget of $5,000-$50,000 per year, Payload CMS is a better choice due to its more flexible pricing model and easier learning curve. However, larger teams with complex content models may prefer Sanity's robust features and scalability. For small teams or startups, Payload CMS's free plan and lower costs make it a more attractive option.

## When to Choose Payload CMS
* If you're a 10-person startup with a simple content model and a budget of $1,000-$5,000 per year, Payload CMS's free plan and ease of use make it a great choice.
* For a 20-person marketing team with a moderate content model and a budget of $5,000-$20,000 per year, Payload CMS's flexible pricing and gentle learning curve are advantages.
* If you're a small agency with 5-10 clients and a budget of $2,000-$10,000 per year, Payload CMS's lower costs and ease of setup make it a good fit.
* For a 50-person SaaS company with a simple content model and a budget of $10,000-$50,000 per year, Payload CMS's scalability and support are sufficient.

## When to Choose Sanity
* If you're a 100-person enterprise with a complex content model and a budget of $50,000-$200,000 per year, Sanity's robust features, scalability, and support make it a better choice.
* For a 20-person development team with a large, distributed content model and a budget of $10,000-$50,000 per year, Sanity's advanced caching and real-time updates are advantages.
* If you're a large agency with 50-100 clients and a budget of $20,000-$100,000 per year, Sanity's tiered pricing and extensive integrations make it a good fit.
* For a 500-person company with a highly customized content model and a budget of $100,000-$500,000 per year, Sanity's enterprise features and support are necessary.
