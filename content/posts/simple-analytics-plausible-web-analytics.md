# Simple Analytics vs Plausible: Which is Better for Web Analytics?

## Quick Verdict
For small to medium-sized teams with a focus on minimalist web analytics, Plausible is the better choice due to its simplicity and cost-effectiveness. However, larger teams with more complex analytics needs may prefer Simple Analytics for its scalability and wider range of features. Ultimately, the choice depends on your team's specific needs and budget.

## When to Choose Simple Analytics
- If you're a 50-person SaaS company needing advanced analytics features like heatmaps and session recordings to optimize your website's user experience, Simple Analytics is the better choice.
- For larger teams with complex analytics needs, such as tracking multiple websites or requiring custom analytics solutions, Simple Analytics offers more flexibility and scalability.
- If you have a large budget and require 24/7 support, Simple Analytics provides more comprehensive support options.
- For example, if you're a 100-person e-commerce company with a large marketing team, Simple Analytics can help you track and analyze your website's performance in detail.

## When to Choose Plausible
- If you're a small startup or solo entrepreneur with a simple website and limited budget, Plausible offers an affordable and easy-to-use analytics solution.
- For teams prioritizing data privacy and simplicity, Plausible's minimalist approach and focus on GDPR compliance make it an attractive choice.
- If you're a 10-person agency managing small to medium-sized client websites, Plausible's ease of use and cost-effectiveness make it a great option for providing basic analytics insights to clients.
- For instance, if you're a 5-person non-profit organization with limited technical expertise, Plausible's simplicity and community-driven support make it a great choice.

## Real-World Use Case: Web Analytics
Let's consider a real-world scenario where a 20-person marketing team needs to track website analytics for a medium-sized e-commerce website. 
- Setup complexity: Simple Analytics requires 2-3 hours to set up, while Plausible can be set up in under 30 minutes.
- Ongoing maintenance burden: Simple Analytics requires regular checks on data accuracy and occasional adjustments to tracking codes, while Plausible is relatively low-maintenance.
- Cost breakdown for 100 users/actions: Simple Analytics would cost around $29/month, while Plausible would cost around $15/month.
- Common gotchas: With Simple Analytics, it's easy to get overwhelmed by the sheer amount of data and features, while with Plausible, the limited features might not be enough for more complex analytics needs.

## Migration Considerations
If switching between these tools:
- Data export/import limitations: Both tools allow data export, but importing data into the other tool may require manual effort or third-party services.
- Training time needed: Simple Analytics requires more training time due to its steeper learning curve, while Plausible is generally easier to learn.
- Hidden costs: When switching from Simple Analytics to Plausible, you might need to adjust your tracking codes, which could require additional development time and costs.

## FAQ
Q: Which tool is better for GDPR compliance?
A: Plausible is more focused on GDPR compliance and data privacy, making it a better choice for teams prioritizing these aspects.

Q: Can I use both together?
A: Yes, you can use both Simple Analytics and Plausible together, but it might require additional setup and integration efforts. This approach can provide a more comprehensive analytics solution, but it also increases costs and complexity.

A: Based on a 12-month projection, Plausible offers a better ROI for small to medium-sized teams, with estimated cost savings of around 30-50% compared to Simple Analytics.
