# Lighthouse vs PageSpeed Insights: Which is Better for Performance?

## Quick Verdict
For small to medium-sized teams with limited budgets, PageSpeed Insights is a more cost-effective option, while larger teams with more complex performance needs may prefer Lighthouse. Ultimately, the choice between the two depends on your team's specific requirements and priorities. If you're looking for more advanced features and a higher degree of customization, Lighthouse may be the better choice.

## When to Choose Lighthouse
- If you're a 50-person SaaS company needing advanced performance auditing and customization, Lighthouse is a better fit, with its highly scalable architecture and extensive integration options.
- For teams with complex, large-scale applications, Lighthouse provides more detailed and customizable performance audits, making it easier to identify and address performance bottlenecks.
- If you require more control over your performance auditing process, Lighthouse's open-source nature and extensive community support make it an attractive option.
- For example, if you're a 20-person e-commerce company with a high-traffic website, Lighthouse can help you optimize your site's performance and improve user experience.

## When to Choose PageSpeed Insights
- If you're a small business or solo developer with limited resources, PageSpeed Insights is a more accessible option, with a gentler learning curve and simpler, more straightforward audits.
- For teams with smaller, less complex applications, PageSpeed Insights provides a more streamlined and user-friendly experience, with easy-to-understand audits and recommendations.
- If you're already invested in the Google ecosystem, with tools like Google Analytics and Google Search Console, PageSpeed Insights integrates seamlessly with these services, making it a convenient choice.
- For instance, if you're a 5-person startup with a simple website, PageSpeed Insights can help you quickly identify and address basic performance issues.

Hidden costs include potential losses in productivity and performance during the migration process, which can be mitigated with careful planning and execution.
