# Jira vs Linear: Which is Better for Issue Tracking?

## Quick Verdict
For large enterprises with complex workflows and deep pockets, Jira is the better choice, offering advanced features and scalability. However, for startups and small teams with simpler workflows, Linear provides a more streamlined and cost-effective solution. Ultimately, the choice between Jira and Linear depends on your team's size, budget, and specific issue tracking needs.

## Feature Comparison Table
| Feature Category | Jira | Linear | Winner |

## When to Choose Jira
* If you're a 500-person enterprise with multiple teams and complex workflows, Jira's advanced features and scalability make it the better choice.
* If you need to integrate with a wide range of third-party apps, Jira's ecosystem is more comprehensive.
* If you're willing to invest time and resources into customizing and configuring Jira, it can provide a high degree of flexibility and control.
* For example, if you're a 50-person SaaS company needing to manage multiple projects and workflows, Jira's advanced features and scalability make it a good fit, despite the higher cost and steeper learning curve.

## When to Choose Linear
* If you're a 10-person startup with simple workflows and limited budget, Linear's streamlined interface and cost-effective pricing make it the better choice.
* If you need to get up and running quickly, Linear's gentle learning curve and intuitive workflows make it easier to onboard new team members.
* If you prioritize ease of use and simplicity, Linear's minimalistic design and automated task assignment features make it a good fit.
* For example, if you're a 20-person dev team needing to manage a single project with simple workflows, Linear's ease of use and cost-effectiveness make it a good choice.

## Real-World Use Case: Issue Tracking
Let's say you're a 50-person SaaS company with multiple teams and projects, and you need to track issues across different workflows. With Jira, setup complexity would take around 2-3 days, with ongoing maintenance burden of 2-3 hours per week. The cost breakdown for 100 users would be around $1,000-1,500 per month. Common gotchas include complexity in configuring custom workflows and integrating with third-party apps. With Linear, setup complexity would take around 1-2 hours, with ongoing maintenance burden of 1 hour per week. The cost breakdown for 100 users would be around $800-1,200 per month. Common gotchas include limited customization options and limited scalability.
