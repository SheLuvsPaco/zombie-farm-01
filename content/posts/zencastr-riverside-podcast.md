# Zencastr vs Riverside: Which is Better for Podcast?

## Quick Verdict
For small to medium-sized podcast teams with a budget under $100 per month, Zencastr is the better choice due to its local recording capabilities and ease of use. However, for larger teams or those requiring more advanced features, Riverside is a better option. Ultimately, the choice between Zencastr and Riverside depends on your team's specific needs and budget.

## When to Choose Zencastr
* If you're a 10-person podcast team with a budget under $50 per month and need a simple, easy-to-use platform for local recording, Zencastr is a great choice.
* If you prioritize ease of use and don't require advanced features like video recording or live streaming, Zencastr is a good option.
* If you're a solo podcaster or a small team looking for a cost-effective solution with good audio quality, Zencastr is a suitable choice.
* For example, if you're a 5-person team creating a weekly podcast with a budget of $20 per month, Zencastr can help you reduce sync time from 15 minutes to 30 seconds.

## When to Choose Riverside
* If you're a 20-person podcast team with a budget over $100 per month and require advanced features like video recording, live streaming, and high-quality audio, Riverside is the better choice.
* If you need to integrate your podcast with other tools like YouTube, Spotify, or Zoom, Riverside offers more integrations.
* If you prioritize high-quality audio and video, Riverside's 48kHz, 24-bit audio and 4K video capabilities make it a great option.
* For instance, if you're a 50-person SaaS company needing to create high-quality video podcasts with live streaming capabilities, Riverside can provide the necessary features and scalability.
