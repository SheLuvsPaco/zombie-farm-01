# Bunny CDN vs Cloudflare: Which is Better for CDN?

## Quick Verdict
For small to medium-sized teams with limited budgets, Bunny CDN is a more cost-effective solution with a simpler pricing model, while larger enterprises may prefer Cloudflare for its advanced security features and extensive integration options. However, if pricing performance is the top priority, Bunny CDN is the better choice. Ultimately, the decision depends on your team's specific needs and use case.

## When to Choose Bunny CDN
- If you're a 10-person startup with a limited budget and need a simple, cost-effective CDN solution, Bunny CDN is a great choice.
- If you're a small e-commerce business using Shopify or WordPress, Bunny CDN's seamless integration and affordable pricing make it an attractive option.
- If you prioritize ease of use and a straightforward pricing model, Bunny CDN is the better choice.
- For example, if you're a 50-person SaaS company needing a reliable CDN for your web application, Bunny CDN's pricing starts at $19/month for 1TB, which can reduce your costs by up to 30% compared to Cloudflare.

## When to Choose Cloudflare
- If you're a large enterprise with complex security requirements, Cloudflare's advanced features, such as DDoS protection and web application firewall, make it a more suitable choice.
- If you need to integrate your CDN with a wide range of platforms, including custom solutions, Cloudflare's extensive integration options are unparalleled.
- If you're willing to invest time in learning and configuring Cloudflare's advanced features, it can provide a more comprehensive CDN solution.
- For instance, if you're a 100-person e-commerce company handling high traffic volumes, Cloudflare's scalability and support features can ensure a more reliable and secure online presence.

## Real-World Use Case: CDN
Let's consider a scenario where a 20-person SaaS company needs to set up a CDN for its web application. With Bunny CDN, the setup process takes around 2 hours, and ongoing maintenance is minimal. The cost breakdown for 100 users and 1,000 actions per day would be approximately $29/month. In contrast, Cloudflare's setup process takes around 5 hours due to its more complex features, and the cost breakdown would be around $40/month. However, Cloudflare's advanced security features and scalability make it a better choice for larger teams or those with complex security requirements.

## Migration Considerations
If switching from Bunny CDN to Cloudflare, data export/import limitations may apply, and training time needed to learn Cloudflare's advanced features can take up to 2 weeks. Hidden costs, such as additional support fees, may also arise. Conversely, switching from Cloudflare to Bunny CDN is relatively straightforward, with minimal data export/import limitations and a shorter training time of around 1 week.
